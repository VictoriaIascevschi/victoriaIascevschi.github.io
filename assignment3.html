To what extent is the division of roles effective? Everything is clear and structured and it enhances the effectiveness. Team roles are provided with a clear understanding of how different personalities and skills can interact together in a team. They are particularly useful for improving team communication and effectiveness in a structured way.
Which roles reinforce each other? Personally, I believe all team roles are connected with each other and complement other team roles. The co-ordinator can help turn the plant's unique ideas into actionable plans, while the plant’s creativity can provide the co-ordinator with fresh perspectives and solutions. What is more, the shaper focuses on results and motivates the implementer, who in turn provides the structured plan of actions and discipline needed to execute the shaper’s ideas. The specialist can provide technical work and bring the plant’s ideas into real tasks, while the plant’s creativity can push the specialist to find new directions for turning ideas into actions.
Which roles may get in each other's way? The plant focuses on creativity and generating new ideas might not be approved by the monitor evaluator because of critical analysis, which may seem overly skeptical or dismissive of unconventional ideas. The resource investigator's duty is to chase new opportunities and start new tasks that might conflict with the completer finisher as he needs to ensure that existing tasks are completed properly and on time.
What can you change to improve collaboration? I can do my best to provide really useful and clear feedback so that our team would know what aspects we have to work on more and put more effort into them. I can also try to set tasks and make sure that they are done on time. In addition, I can do research and find rare information which can come in handy while working on a project. Also, support team members and provide help when it is needed.
