10b. Open question

Describe one value that is very applicable to you and substantiate your choice with a personal example.

Your answer

One value that I find very important and useful is discipline. Discipline is the ability to stay committed to a goal, be consistent, and push through difficulties even when motivation fades. It is what transforms ambitions into achievements and helps go through life's challenges with resilience and determination. When I entered the university I started to develop my discipline. Now we have weekly assignments from some of our subjects and projects. I set my own deadlines for these assignments and try to do them earlier in order to have more free time at the weekends. This way, I can have more free time and I don't need to worry that I am running out of time as I always have some extra time if I need to do any corrections. Also, when I first started learning to code at the beginning of the study year, I was excited and highly motivated. However, as the concepts became more complex, I often felt overwhelmed and frustrated. There were times when I wanted to give up because I didn't understand a particular topic, I was struggling when I was writing code and there were lots of mistakes. What kept me going was discipline. I made a structured study plan, committing to learning at least an hour every day(apart from weekly tutorials at university), regardless of my mood or external distractions. Even on days when I felt tired or discouraged, I reminded myself that progress is made through persistence, not just bursts of motivation. Over time, I saw gradual improvement. Concepts that once seemed impossible became easier, and I eventually reached a level where I could solve complex problems with confidence. This experience taught me that discipline is more than just following a routine, it’s about building mental strength and determination. It enabled me to develop problem-solving skills, patience, and confidence in my abilities.

12d. Table question

What do you want to change in terms of your priorities? Motivate why and describe how.

Your answer

I would like to improve the way my team works. I am the project leader so I decided that it would be a nice idea to use a structured approach like weekly progress meetings. Helping teammates is important, but sometimes requests can be delegated or scheduled for later if they are non-urgent so that we can focus on more important and urgent tasks. In addition, I always encourage my teammates to sit together so that we can help each other if it is needed on the spot without postponing the troubles. As a result, everyone feels free to ask for help and share their thoughts without slowing down the whole team's work progress. To my mind, handling team-related urgencies more strategically leads to better time management, stronger team efficiency, and reduced stress. By implementing a structured working plan, encouraging problem-solving way, and setting clear availability, I can contribute to teamwork without sacrificing my own priorities.

I would have liked to say no to frequently checking and responding to non-urgent emails which sometimes distracted me from more important work and I had to waste time on useless things. By setting boundaries, I can focus on important tasks without unnecessary stress. I don't mind helping others but firstly I need to finish my work which is urgent and important and then I can help if it is actually needed.
