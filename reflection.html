The Importance of Reflection

Learning outcome

You now know what reflection entails and you have argued why it is useful for you to develop this skill further in work or study situations.

Question 1: Which of the three above-mentioned instruments are used in your study programme?

In my study programme is used Personal Development Plan. Every period we work on different projects and we have supervisors who can answer our questions but mostly we have to do research on our own and concentrate on self-study. As a result, individuals understand their skills, interests, and ambitions, and then set goals for their personal and professional development. PDP helps evaluate personal strengths, weaknesses, values, and skills. In addition, at the end of the second period of the first year, we have to make a portfolio for professional skills. In a portfolio, we will put together the necessary documents and record our reflections on the most important experiences. This allows us to periodically look back on how we have developed and check the progress. It also serves as proof of our development towards our educators and they are also able to see how we gain more experience and skills.

Question 2: Conclusion: substantiate the extent to which learning to reflect more effectively is important for you and your study programme and/or work.

In my opinion, learning to reflect is really important for me and my study programme because it provides students an opportunity to deepen their thoughts, improve their learning process, and master ideas. After learning tasks, if students are encouraged to reflect on their own work, they'll better retain their full engagement in class which also helps them remember concepts over a longer period of time. In this way, self-reflection helps us improve our memory and keeps our minds active, and it is ideal for looking back on learning. In addition, you can see your personal growth and check the progress of your own development and how you learn and gain new skills. Your motivation for studying can be improved if you reflect on your study goals and relate them to the broader goals in your life, both personal and professional. Reflection can help you to define immediate goals and then devise strategies to achieve them. Getting used to reflecting on your experiences should be a part of your everyday learning. In this way, each experience whether positive or negative will contribute to my development and personal growth.
