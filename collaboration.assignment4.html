Group assignment

22. Table Question

Describe the common goals of a collaboration that you are currently part of.
Our common goal is to raise awareness about the deaf community and how they experience the world, highlighting the challenges that they face daily, about which normal people might have never thought. We are doing this by combining our university innovation project with a volunteering project, MDT. We have already built a website where deaf people can share their stories and are developing a VR game to give people an immersive experience of what it feels like to be deaf and give them a chance to experience the problems that deaf people face, and how sometimes it's difficult to manage them.
Is the common goal aligned within the group? Do all group members have a similar understanding of the common goal?
Yes, everyone in our group understands and supports the goal. We came up with this idea by ourselves, and all agreed that helping the deaf community and spreading awareness is important. We each bring different skills to the project, and we are all working toward the same purpose.
What individual goals do you wish to achieve through this collaboration?
I want to improve my skills in teamwork, communication, and creativity. I also aim to strengthen my abilities in coding and design. In addition, I want to better understand real-world challenges and explore how technology, such as virtual reality, can be used to help solve social issues. Most importantly, I hope to make a positive difference in people’s lives through this project.
What are the individual goals of the other group members?
The other group members also want to grow their technical and creative skills. Some are focused on improving their coding and 3D design abilities, while others are working on communication and project coordination. Everyone wants to create something meaningful that helps raise awareness about the deaf community, while also learning how to build a strong project together.
Substantiate to what extent your individual goals are in line with the common goals.
My individual goals fit very well with our common goal. I want to learn how to use technology like VR to solve social problems, and our project is doing exactly that — using VR to show what it feels like to be deaf. I also want to improve my teamwork and design skills, which I’m doing by contributing to the website and the game. So both my learning and personal goals support and benefit the whole project.

24. Open question

Reflection question: to what extent do you agree that the common goals in a collaboration should take precedence over individual goals?

Your answer

In my opinion, the team’s common goals in most cases take priority and are more important in a collaboration than individual ones. When a team works toward a shared purpose, aligning everyone’s efforts creates stronger results and helps avoid confusion or conflict. Common goals provide direction, keep the group focused, and ensure that everyone contributes to the overall success of the project. However, individual goals also matter and should be set and achieved. When team members can pursue personal growth by learning more deeply, specifically what they find interesting for themselves, and gaining more experience, they tend to be more motivated and committed. The best collaborations find a balance, prioritizing shared goals while also supporting individual development where possible. In short, common goals should lead, but individual goals should not be ignored. They can coexist and even strengthen one another.
