Feedback

Learning outcome

You are able to indicate the importance of feedback for learning and good collaboration and are able to give and receive feedback in accordance with the established (theoretical) rules.

Question 1: Describe the situation.

I have noticed that sometimes you behave impatiently and interrupt when any of our team members are saying something as you don't give them the opportunity to finish their sentence. I know that you are hard-working and I understand that you want to share your ideas and thoughts immediately but it cuts the conversation and disregards the current speaker and even sometimes he/she might feel annoyed. Also, it seems that you sometimes miss what we are saying and it makes it difficult to communicate effectively because I or other team members try to help you but you don't take into consideration our words and efforts. I would like you to be more polite and appreciate other team members because sometimes we can have conflicts and if we don't listen to each other, working in a group will be impossible.

Behaviour Your answer(s)
Behaviour You sometimes behave impatiently and interrupt other team members when they are speaking, not allowing them to finish their sentences. Also, you occasionally miss important points in the conversation, even when the team tries to help.
Emotion This behavior cuts off the conversation, disregards the current speaker, and may cause frustration or annoyance among team members. It also leads to misunderstandings, making effective communication difficult, and spoils the harmony in the team.
Consequence As a result, the lack of respect for others' input can lead to conflicts, as team members may feel undervalued or disrespected. By not fully listening to others, important information may be missed, making it harder to collaborate and communicate effectively. Also, interruptions and failure to listen can cause team members to feel frustrated or ignored, leading to tension within the group.
Desirable I hope that you become more patient, polite, and considerate. This includes listening fully to others before sharing your thoughts and taking into account what your other teammates are saying. By improving your listening skills and appreciating other team members’ input, the number of conflicts can be reduced, and communication with the team will improve. This will create a more comfortable environment and it will be more pleasant to work together and finally, everyone will get on well with each other.

    Giving feedback

    Question 4:

  • What was it like to give the other person feedback?
  • To what extent have you succeeded in conveying the core of the feedback to the other?
  • insights from this section have helped you with this?
  • Is the other person going to do something with the feedback?

Personally, I believe giving feedback, especially when it is a tip feedback, can feel challenging because you need to feel a balance between addressing the issues and being respectful and supportive. However, using the BECD method made it a bit easier to give feedback, and following the structure helps to make the feedback specific and clear and not to miss any important points. It helped break down the situation into manageable parts: identifying specific behaviors, explaining the impact, suggesting changes, and outlining desired outcomes. This approach makes the feedback easier to understand and allows the recipient to see both the problems and potential solutions. I am sure that it is important to know how to give feedback as the key to success while working in a group is communication. What is more, it is very useful experience because you can improve your communication skills and make working in a group more efficient as if team members receive and follow the feedback working together will become easier and more effective as they will also develop their skills and gain new knowledge. I really learnt a lot from this section and got lots of useful advice on how to give feedback properly and make it effective. Also, how to receive it and how it is important to listen to it and accept it. In my opinion, now I am better at giving feedback as I am really trying to help other team members and encourage them to become professionals by developing their skills. We already had the class where we gave feedback to each other and I enjoyed it as we are all interested in the results of our project and we are ready to change and develop our skills and do our best. I hope that the feedback which I shared with my team members was useful for them and they will listen to my advice and maybe soon I will see their results and improvements.

  • What are you going to do with this feedback?

I have already received feedback from my teammate and he advised me to communicate more with other team members in order to strengthen our relationships with the whole team. We have been working together for 6 weeks in one group and I agree that with some team members, I communicate more than with others. Now I am trying to talk with everyone equally and I can see that it is becoming easier to work in a group because we know each other better and we feel more comfortable sharing our opinions and thoughts. I took the feedback into account and I am already trying to be more open to team members and talk with them more not only on topics related to the project but also on some other free topics. I really enjoyed this feedback and I really want to get it from my team members in the future too because feedbacks help me to become better and work on myself. I think it is important to hear the feedback from people around me as it helps to understand better what I have to work on more.
