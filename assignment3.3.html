    Question 2: What are your most important reasons not to go to class? Argue why you think this is good reason or not.

    The reasons for not attending class can vary greatly depending on personal circumstances, priorities, and values. Physical illness or mental health challenges might make attending class difficult and not useful. It's difficult to concentrate and work when you are sick because you usually feel tired and your brain is not able to work properly. Also, as a reason could be that if the class feels irrelevant to my goals or the teaching style does not engage me. This means that the time spent could be better invested in self-study or other productive activities. For instance, if I can learn the material more efficiently through online resources(e.g. watching video tutorials, or reading a book), skipping a poorly taught lecture might make sense. It might be that the teacher uses inappropriate teaching techniques which makes the lesson ineffective and not understandable for students. However, there are some cases where skipping class is a reasonable choice, consistent attendance is usually a better approach.

Question 4: What are your most important reasons to go to class? Explain to what extent these reasons already encourage you to go to class.

Personally, I think there are lots of reasons why attending class is beneficial and important. Attending class allows me to directly engage with the instructor during his lecture, ask questions, and receive clarification on specific topics. Asking questions during the teacher's explanation is quite effective and it makes the complex topics more clear and understandable. The opportunity to interact in real-time and gain insights beyond textbooks strongly motivates me to attend, especially for subjects I find challenging. In addition, getting advice from experienced teachers is really useful because you can know exactly what is waiting for you in your future job and you can be more prepared and aware of the challenges which you might face. A class provides a structured schedule and pace for covering material, which helps in staying consistent with studies. This helps me stay on track and classes often push me to study in order not to fall apart and manage the time correctly to have enough time to cover all topics. Group discussions, brainstorming, and sharing ideas in class offer perspectives and broaden your knowledge. Attending class provides insights into what is expected during exams, including hints, examples, or practical demonstrations. This is a major motivator because instructors often emphasize key points or clarify exam-related doubts. In addition, regular attendance of classes makes me more organised and responsible. I'm trying to be consistent and I know that only after hard work and lots of effort, I will see the results. The reasons above strongly encourage me to attend class, particularly when it comes to gaining clarity on difficult topics and staying prepared for assessments or exams. However, their influence can vary depending on the relevance of the session and the instructor's teaching style.

Question 5: Substantiate the extent to which you make effective notes during class and use the theory and feedback to describe at least two things you will be doing starting tomorrow to make your notes more effective.

I was mostly used to taking notes on paper with a pen because we weren't allowed to use laptops while studying at school. However, now I quite often type on my laptop instead of writing with a pen. Now I want to come back to writing my notes by pen during lectures. I will use the advice form this module and during lectures, I will describe important concepts as briefly as possible in my own words. In addition, after the lesson, I will arrange my notes improving them with additional information and it will be easier to study those notes again before the test. What is more, I will process new information more effectively as it will stick to my memory and I will remember that information for a longer period. Also, I will be more focused during the lessons because I will think about the new information and process it properly. As a result, it will be easier to understand it and structure it in my head.
